Project profile

About Fractal Lab

Fractal Lab was created by Hannah Grewcock, a Master's student at the University of St Andrews, as an interactive way to explore fractal geometry through visual experiments, adjustable parameters, and live mathematical examples.

Creator Hannah Grewcock

Motivation

Why this site exists

Fractal Lab was created from the belief that fractal geometry becomes clearer when it can be seen, changed, and explored directly. Many fractals begin with simple rules, but their behaviour can be difficult to understand through static diagrams alone. Interactive visualisation helps make these ideas more intuitive.

The project brings together examples from iterated function systems, affine maps, complex dynamics, fractal curves, stochastic growth, and three-dimensional constructions. Each section acts as a small workspace where users can adjust parameters, compare examples, and connect formulas with the images they produce.

The aim is to make fractal geometry more approachable without losing the mathematical structure behind it. Fractal Lab is intended as a learning and outreach tool: a place for students, teachers, and curious users to experiment, build intuition, and engage with the subject visually.
